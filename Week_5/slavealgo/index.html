Slave To The Algorithm Exercise

COIN FLIP COIN FLIP

Our homework in preparation for week 5 was to become "slaves" to the algorithm. We were told to let chance dictate our actions.

For my experiment, I took a coin flip website and used it to choose the way I would travel to campus from Flinders Station. A 50/50 coin flip chose if I was going to walk or if I was going to use the tram, and this flip would occur every time I stopped at a tram stop.

Surprisingly, despite each chance being a 50/50 flip, I got way more tails than heads. This meant that I had to "tuck my tail" and run for the next stop more than I could easily "head forward" with the tram. It definitely was not the optimal method of getting to campus, but it did alleviate my brain from having to make the choice of "tram" or "walk".
